### I. Energy Unlocks Everything

**The dependency graph:**
- Food production → energy (irrigation, fertilizer, transport)
- Clean water → energy (desalination, pumping, treatment)
- Computation → energy (data centers, training runs)
- Manufacturing → energy (refinement, fabrication, assembly)
- Healthcare → energy (hospitals, equipment, refrigeration)
- Transportation → energy (obvious)

**Thesis:** Energy is not "a resource among resources." It's the master resource that unlocks all others.

### II. The Universe Floods Us With Energy

**Solar abundance:**
- 173,000 TW striking Earth continuously
- 10,000x current human consumption
- One hour of sunlight > one year of human energy use

**Fusion potential:**
- Deuterium in oceans: 26 billion years at current rates
- Helium-3 on Moon: centuries of energy
- Jupiter atmosphere: effectively unlimited

### III. Cost Curves Are Collapsing

**Solar:**
- 90% cost decline in 10 years
- Still falling exponentially
- Grid parity achieved in most markets

**Battery storage:**
- 89% cost decline since 2010
- Enables intermittent renewables
- Still improving rapidly

**Fusion:**
- NIF achieved ignition (2022)
- Multiple private companies targeting 2030s
- Not "50 years away" anymore

### IV. What Energy Abundance Changes

**From scarcity thinking to possibility thinking:**

When energy is scarce:
- Zero-sum trade-offs
- Conflict over resources
- Short-term survival dominates
- Suffering rationalized as inevitable

When energy is abundant:
- Positive-sum opportunities
- Infrastructure > competition
- Long-term thinking possible
- Cruelty becomes unjustifiable

<PullQuote>

Abundance doesn't guarantee good choices. But it removes the false excuse that cruelty is economically necessary.

</PullQuote>

### V. Specific Unlocks

**Desalination:**
- Cheap energy → unlimited fresh water
- Every ocean becomes a water source
- Agriculture transforms

**Vertical farming:**
- Energy-intensive but land-efficient
- Feed billions without destroying forests
- Controlled environments, zero pesticides

**Carbon capture:**
- Currently too expensive (energy cost)
- With abundant energy: reverses climate change while transitioning

**Manufacturing:**
- Recycling becomes economical (energy for separation)
- On-site production (energy > shipping)
- Eliminates dangerous/degrading labor (automation)

### VI. Infrastructure Is the Bottleneck

**The pattern:**
- Nature provides abundance (sun, fusion fuel)
- We create scarcity (lack of capture/conversion/distribution)
- Physics permits it
- Infrastructure determines access

**What's needed:**
- Solar capture and storage
- Fusion containment
- Distribution networks
- Political will

### VII. The Moral Dimension Revisited

**Energy access = human development:**
- 770M people lack electricity
- 2.6B lack clean cooking fuel
- Both solvable with today's technology
- Every delay = millions trapped in poverty

**Historical pattern:**
- Societies become more humane as they become wealthier
- Not because wealth = virtue
- But because abundance removes pressures that make cruelty seem necessary

### VIII. The Age of Wonders Runs on Energy

**Close with:**
- SGL requires energy (station-keeping, data processing)
- Free Starship requires energy (fusion propulsion)
- Programmable matter requires energy (computation, manufacturing)
- Everything cascades from energy

**Setup for Essay 7:** With abundant energy and programmable matter, exploration transforms. Supply chains disappear. Mobility becomes lightweight.
